7 Common AC Problems Pasadena Homeowners Face

Expert troubleshooting guide to help you identify and solve air conditioning issues in Pasadena's unique climate

Air conditioning systems in Pasadena face unique challenges due to our Southern California climate and local environmental factors. From the scorching heat waves that can push temperatures into the upper 90s to the Santa Ana winds that bring dust and debris, Pasadena's HVAC systems work harder than in many other regions.

As a local HVAC company serving Pasadena for over 15 years, we've diagnosed and repaired thousands of air conditioning problems. We've found that certain issues are particularly common in our area, and understanding these problems can help you identify them early, potentially saving money on repairs and avoiding uncomfortable days without cooling.

In this guide, we'll walk through the 7 most common AC problems Pasadena homeowners face, explain their causes, and provide troubleshooting steps you can take. We'll also clarify when a problem requires professional attention – because while some issues have simple DIY fixes, others can worsen if not properly addressed by a qualified technician.

Pasadena Climate Challenges for AC Systems

Pasadena's inland location creates more temperature extremes than coastal areas, with summer temperatures regularly reaching the mid-90s. Additionally, the region's occasional Santa Ana winds bring hot, dry conditions and increased dust that can affect AC performance. During fire season, smoke particles can clog filters more quickly. These factors combine to create a demanding environment for air conditioning systems.

Problem #1: AC Not Cooling Properly

One of the most common complaints we receive from Pasadena homeowners is that their air conditioner is running but not cooling effectively. During our hot summer months, this problem is particularly frustrating and can make homes uncomfortably warm.

Symptoms
  • System runs continuously but doesn't reach the set temperature
  • Airflow seems normal, but the air isn't sufficiently cool
  • Some rooms are cool while others remain warm
  • Temperature difference between supply and return air is less than 14-20 degrees

Common Causes in Pasadena Homes

Several factors specific to our region contribute to cooling problems:

  • Undersized System for Pasadena Heat: Many older Pasadena homes have AC systems that were sized based on historical temperatures, not accounting for the increasing heat we've experienced in recent years.
  • Refrigerant Leaks: The intense summer heat causes systems to work harder, potentially stressing refrigerant lines and leading to leaks over time.
  • Dirty Condenser Coils: Pasadena's Santa Ana winds and urban dust can quickly clog outdoor condenser units, reducing their ability to release heat.
  • Restricted Airflow: Ductwork issues common in Pasadena's older homes can restrict proper airflow.
Solutions:
DIY Troubleshooting:
  • Check and replace air filters (recommended monthly during peak summer)
  • Clear debris from around outdoor unit (maintain 2 feet of clearance)
  • Ensure all supply and return vents are open and unobstructed
  • Check thermostat settings and battery (if applicable)
  • Clean accessible vents and registers
When to Call a Professional:
  • If DIY measures don't improve cooling
  • If you suspect a refrigerant leak (requires EPA certification to handle)
  • If your system is more than 10 years old and struggling with Pasadena heat
  • If you notice ice formation on refrigerant lines

Problem #2: System Constantly Cycles On and Off

Short cycling—when your AC turns on and off frequently rather than running in normal cooling cycles—is particularly common during Pasadena's hottest months. This problem not only affects your comfort but also significantly increases energy consumption and system wear.

Symptoms
  • AC runs for brief periods (less than 10 minutes) before shutting off
  • System turns on and off more than 3-4 times per hour
  • Home temperature fluctuates noticeably
  • Increased utility bills despite seemingly less run time

Common Causes in Pasadena Homes

Several factors make this issue prevalent in our area:

  • Oversized AC Systems: Some Pasadena homes have improperly sized systems that cool too quickly without adequately removing humidity.
  • Dirty Air Filters: During wildfire season, Pasadena's air quality issues can clog filters much faster than normal.
  • Thermostat Location Issues: Many Pasadena homes have thermostats installed on west-facing walls that receive afternoon sun, causing false readings.
  • Low Refrigerant: The extended cooling season in Pasadena can lead to small refrigerant leaks over time.
Solutions:
DIY Troubleshooting:
  • Replace air filters (use MERV 8-11 during wildfire season)
  • Check thermostat placement—shield from direct sunlight or relocate if necessary
  • Ensure outdoor unit is clean and unobstructed
  • Check that indoor registers aren't blocked by furniture or drapes
When to Call a Professional:
  • If short cycling continues after DIY attempts
  • If you suspect electrical issues or control board problems
  • If your system is significantly oversized for your home
  • To check refrigerant levels (this requires specialized equipment)

Problem #3: Water Leaking from Indoor Unit

Water leakage from indoor AC units is especially common in Pasadena during the transition periods between dry and more humid conditions. This issue can damage ceilings, walls, and floors if not addressed quickly.

Symptoms
  • Water dripping from or collecting around indoor air handler
  • Water stains on ceiling or walls near the air handler
  • Musty smell near indoor unit
  • System may shut down if float switch is triggered

Common Causes in Pasadena Homes

Several factors specific to our region contribute to this problem:

  • Condensate Drain Clogs: Pasadena's alternating dry and humid conditions create ideal environments for algae growth in drain lines.
  • Improper Installation: Many older Pasadena homes have AC units retrofitted in attics without proper drain pan installations.
  • Dirty Air Filters: Restricted airflow from dirty filters can cause evaporator coils to freeze and then thaw, creating excess water.
  • Disconnected Drain Lines: Pasadena's occasional minor earthquakes can sometimes disconnect condensate drain lines.
Solutions:
DIY Troubleshooting:
  • Replace air filter if dirty
  • Clear visible blockages from the condensate drain line
  • Pour 1 cup of white vinegar down the drain line monthly during cooling season (if accessible)
  • Ensure the drain line is properly sloped for gravity flow
When to Call a Professional:
  • If water continues to leak after DIY attempts
  • If you can't access the drain line easily
  • If you notice water damage to surrounding areas
  • If the system is shutting down due to float switch activation

Warning: Water Damage Risk

Water leaks can cause significant structural damage and mold growth, particularly in Pasadena's older homes with plaster ceilings and walls. Don't delay addressing this issue, as repairs for water damage often exceed the cost of a simple AC repair.

Problem #4: Unusual Noises from AC System

Strange noises from your air conditioning system often signal developing problems. Identifying these sounds can help pinpoint issues before they lead to system failures—particularly important during Pasadena's heat waves when replacement parts may be backordered due to high demand.

Symptoms
  • Grinding or Screeching: Metal-on-metal sounds from the blower or fan motors
  • Rattling or Buzzing: Loose components or debris in the system
  • Clicking: Electrical issues or failing components
  • Hissing: Potential refrigerant leaks
  • Banging or Thumping: Blower assembly problems or loose parts

Common Causes in Pasadena Homes

Several factors make noise issues common in our area:

  • Extended Run Times: During Pasadena heat waves, systems run longer, accelerating wear on bearings and motors.
  • Debris from Santa Ana Winds: Strong winds blow debris into outdoor units, causing rattling and potential damage.
  • Voltage Fluctuations: Southern California power grid strain during peak summer usage can cause electrical component issues.
  • Age-Related Wear: Many Pasadena homes have AC systems approaching or exceeding their expected lifespan.
Solutions:
DIY Troubleshooting:
  • Turn system off before investigating to prevent injury
  • Remove debris around outdoor unit
  • Tighten visible loose screws or panels (with system off)
  • Replace air filter if dirty
When to Call a Professional:
  • For any grinding, screeching, or metal-on-metal sounds
  • If hissing sounds indicate possible refrigerant leaks
  • For persistent electrical clicking noises
  • If noises continue after basic DIY checks

Noise Identification Tip

Record the noise with your smartphone before calling a technician. This can help them diagnose the problem more accurately and arrive with the correct replacement parts—particularly helpful during Pasadena's peak summer season when wait times for repairs may be longer.

Problem #5: AC Blowing Warm Air

When your air conditioner blows warm air instead of cool air during Pasadena's hot summer months, it can quickly make your home unbearable. This common problem has several potential causes, and identifying the right one is crucial for proper repair.

Symptoms
  • Air from vents is room temperature or warm
  • System runs continuously but doesn't cool
  • Outdoor unit may be running, but indoor air handler performs poorly
  • Home temperature continues to rise despite system operation

Common Causes in Pasadena Homes

Several region-specific factors contribute to this problem:

  • Refrigerant Leaks: Pasadena's hot climate causes systems to operate under high pressure, increasing leak potential.
  • Compressor Issues: Extended operation during heat waves can lead to compressor failures.
  • Condenser Coil Problems: Dusty conditions in the San Gabriel Valley can cause severe coil fouling.
  • Thermostat or Control Board Failures: Power fluctuations during peak electrical demand periods can damage electronic components.
Solutions:
DIY Troubleshooting:
  • Check thermostat settings - ensure it's set to "Cool" mode
  • Verify that outdoor unit fan is running
  • Check circuit breakers for tripped breakers to outdoor unit
  • Clean or replace air filters
  • Remove debris from around outdoor unit
When to Call a Professional:
  • If outdoor unit isn't running despite power
  • If you suspect refrigerant issues (requires EPA certification)
  • If circuit breakers continue to trip
  • If DIY troubleshooting doesn't resolve the issue

Problem #6: High Energy Bills Despite Normal Usage

Many Pasadena homeowners experience unexpectedly high electricity bills during summer months, even when their usage patterns haven't changed. This often indicates AC efficiency issues that can be addressed to improve performance and reduce costs.

Symptoms
  • Sudden increase in electricity bills
  • AC runs longer than in previous years
  • System struggles to maintain comfortable temperatures
  • Energy usage is significantly higher compared to neighbors with similar homes

Common Causes in Pasadena Homes

Several factors specific to our region contribute to efficiency problems:

  • Ductwork Leaks: Many Pasadena homes, especially those built before the 1980s, have aging ductwork with significant leakage.
  • Inadequate Insulation: Older Pasadena homes often have insufficient attic insulation for our climate zone.
  • System Age: AC units more than 10 years old are significantly less efficient than modern systems.
  • Improper Refrigerant Charge: Systems operating in Pasadena's heat with incorrect refrigerant levels use up to 30% more energy.
Solutions:
DIY Troubleshooting:
  • Replace air filters monthly during peak summer
  • Use ceiling fans to improve air circulation and raise thermostat settings
  • Seal obvious gaps around windows and doors
  • Ensure vents aren't blocked by furniture
  • Use window coverings on south and west-facing windows
When to Call a Professional:
  • For a comprehensive energy audit of your Pasadena home
  • To inspect and seal ductwork (average Pasadena home loses 20-30% of conditioned air through leaky ducts)
  • For proper refrigerant level testing
  • To evaluate if your system needs upgrading (especially if 15+ years old)

Problem #7: Musty Odors or Poor Air Quality

Indoor air quality issues are particularly common in Pasadena homes during summer months due to our unique climate conditions. Musty smells, increased dust, or allergy symptoms often indicate AC-related air quality problems.

Symptoms
  • Musty or moldy odors when AC runs
  • Increased dust accumulation throughout home
  • Family members experiencing more allergy symptoms indoors
  • Visible mold or mildew near air vents

Common Causes in Pasadena Homes

Several factors make air quality issues prevalent in our area:

  • Microbial Growth: Pasadena's alternating dry and humid periods create ideal conditions for mold growth in AC systems.
  • Dirty Air Ducts: Many older Pasadena homes have decades of dust accumulation in ductwork.
  • Wildfire Season: During fire season, outdoor smoke particles infiltrate homes and strain filtration systems.
  • Inadequate Filtration: Standard filters are often insufficient for Pasadena's air quality challenges.
Solutions:
DIY Troubleshooting:
  • Replace air filters with higher MERV rating filters (MERV 8-11 recommended for Pasadena)
  • Clean visible mold from accessible vents and registers
  • Ensure humidity levels stay below 50% (use dehumidifier if necessary)
  • Pour vinegar down condensate drain line monthly to prevent microbial growth
When to Call a Professional:
  • For professional duct cleaning (recommended every 3-5 years in Pasadena)
  • To inspect and clean evaporator coils
  • To install advanced air purification systems (especially beneficial during wildfire season)
  • If musty odors persist despite DIY efforts

Health Concern

Persistent musty odors may indicate mold growth inside your HVAC system, which can cause respiratory issues and allergic reactions. If anyone in your household has asthma, allergies, or respiratory conditions, professional assessment is particularly important.

How to Prevent Common AC Problems in Pasadena

Preventing air conditioning problems is always more cost-effective than repairing them, especially in Pasadena's demanding climate. Here are our top recommendations for maintaining your system and avoiding the common issues we've discussed:

1. Schedule Bi-Annual Professional Maintenance

For Pasadena homes, we recommend two professional maintenance visits annually: one in spring before cooling season and another in fall before heating season. Our 21-point inspection includes:

  • Refrigerant level and pressure testing
  • Electrical component inspection and tightening
  • Condenser and evaporator coil cleaning
  • Blower motor inspection and lubrication
  • Condensate drain line cleaning and treatment
  • Thermostat calibration and programming check
  • Air filter replacement

2. Implement Monthly DIY Maintenance

Between professional visits, these monthly checks can prevent many common problems:

  • Check and replace air filters (monthly during peak summer)
  • Clear debris from around outdoor unit
  • Listen for unusual noises during system startup
  • Pour 1 cup of white vinegar down condensate drain (if accessible)
  • Clean return air grilles and supply registers

3. Upgrade Your Filtration System

Standard 1-inch filters are often inadequate for Pasadena's air quality challenges, especially during wildfire season. Consider upgrading to:

  • Media filters (4-5 inch thick filters that last 6-12 months)
  • Electronic air cleaners
  • UV light purification systems

These upgrades not only improve air quality but also reduce strain on your system and prevent many common problems.

4. Consider Smart Thermostat Installation

Modern smart thermostats can significantly improve efficiency and provide early warning of developing problems. Benefits include:

  • Optimized run times based on Pasadena's weather patterns
  • Early warning alerts when performance declines
  • Remote monitoring capabilities
  • Humidity tracking

When to Call a Professional

While some AC issues can be resolved with DIY troubleshooting, others require professional attention. Call HVAC Pasadena immediately if you experience any of these situations:

Call a Pro Immediately If:

  • Electrical Issues: Circuit breakers trip repeatedly, burning smells, or sparking
  • Refrigerant Leaks: Hissing sounds or ice formation on refrigerant lines
  • Complete System Failure: AC won't turn on at all
  • Grinding or Screeching Noises: Indicates potential motor failure
  • Water Damage: Visible water leakage or ceiling/wall damage
  • Sudden Spike in Energy Bills: 30%+ increase without usage changes

Remember that during Pasadena's peak summer months, HVAC companies get extremely busy. Addressing small problems early can help you avoid emergency service calls during heat waves when wait times may be longer.
