What Constitutes an HVAC Emergency?

Not all HVAC issues require emergency service, but these situations should be addressed immediately:

  • Complete system failure during extreme weather (above 90°F or below at freezing temperatures)
  • Burning odors coming from any part of your HVAC system
  • Gas leaks or suspected gas leaks (leave the premises immediately and call from outside)
  • Carbon monoxide detector alerts or symptoms of CO poisoning
  • Electrical issues such as sparking, smoking, or repeatedly tripping breakers
  • Water leakage that threatens property damage or electrical components
  • Unusual loud noises like banging, grinding, or screeching

If you're experiencing any of these issues, don't wait—call our emergency line immediately at (626) 555-1234.

Common HVAC Emergency Scenarios

Real situations where our emergency service has saved the day for Pasadena residents

Summer AC Failure

When temperatures climb above 95°F in Pasadena and your AC completely stops working, it's more than an inconvenience—it can be dangerous, especially for elderly residents, children, and pets. Our emergency team prioritizes calls during heat waves to prevent heat-related illnesses.

Winter Heating Crisis

When temperatures drop and your furnace fails to ignite or blows cold air, your home can quickly become uncomfortably cold. Our emergency technicians respond promptly to restore heat, preventing frozen pipes and ensuring your family's comfort and safety.

Restaurant HVAC Failure

When a restaurant's HVAC system fails during peak dining hours, it can lead to lost revenue and unhappy customers. Our commercial emergency team understands the urgency and provides priority service to get your business back to comfortable conditions quickly.

Water Leak Emergency

When your AC's condensate line clogs or your drain pan cracks, water can leak into ceilings, walls, and floors, causing significant damage. Our emergency team responds quickly to stop the leak, prevent water damage, and repair the underlying issue.

Electrical HVAC Emergency

Burning smells, sparking, or repeatedly tripping circuit breakers indicate potentially dangerous electrical problems that require immediate attention. Our emergency technicians are trained to safely diagnose and repair electrical issues in HVAC systems.

Carbon Monoxide Concerns

Carbon monoxide leaks from damaged furnaces or heat exchangers present a life-threatening emergency. If your CO detector sounds or you suspect a leak, leave immediately and call from outside. Our emergency team will respond with specialized equipment to detect and resolve the issue.

Emergency HVAC Service FAQs

Answers to common questions about our emergency services

What is your response time for HVAC emergencies in Pasadena?
We guarantee a 60-minute response time for all emergency calls within Pasadena and immediate surrounding areas. Our average response time is actually 42 minutes. For areas slightly farther from our central location, response times may be 60-90 minutes depending on traffic and weather conditions. We always prioritize calls based on safety concerns, with potential gas leaks and carbon monoxide issues receiving top priority.
How much extra does emergency service cost compared to regular service?
Our emergency diagnostic fee is $99 during standard hours and $149 during after-hours, weekends, and holidays. This fee is waived if you proceed with repairs. While our after-hours labor rates are approximately 30-40% higher than standard rates, we never charge "emergency premiums" beyond our published rates. Maintenance plan members receive discounts of 15-35% on all emergency service charges, making it a valuable investment for homeowners.
Do your emergency technicians carry parts for common repairs?
Yes, our emergency service vehicles are stocked with over 300 of the most commonly needed HVAC repair parts, including capacitors, contactors, igniters, flame sensors, circuit boards, motors, and refrigerant. This allows us to complete approximately 85% of emergency repairs in a single visit. For specialized parts, we can often source them through our extensive supplier network, even during weekends and holidays.
How do I know if my HVAC issue qualifies as an emergency?
We consider an HVAC situation an emergency if: 1) You have no heating during freezing temperatures or no cooling during extreme heat (over 90°F); 2) There are safety concerns such as strange odors, gas leaks, or carbon monoxide detector alerts; 3) Your system is causing or threatening water damage; 4) Electrical issues like sparking or smoke are present. If you're unsure, call our emergency line and our team will help determine the appropriate response.
Do you offer emergency commercial HVAC service?
Yes, we provide 24/7 emergency service for commercial properties throughout Pasadena, including restaurants, retail spaces, offices, medical facilities, and industrial buildings. Our commercial emergency team is specially trained to work on larger commercial systems and understands the critical nature of business HVAC needs. We offer priority response for businesses where comfort directly impacts operations.
What should I do while waiting for the emergency technician to arrive?
While waiting for our technician: 1) For safety issues like gas smells or carbon monoxide concerns, evacuate the building immediately and call from outside; 2) For water leaks, place containers to catch dripping water and move valuable items; 3) For cooling emergencies, close blinds, use fans, and stay hydrated; 4) For heating emergencies, use safe space heaters and wear warm clothing; 5) Gather any information about recent HVAC issues or maintenance to share with the technician. Our dispatch team can provide specific guidance for your situation.
